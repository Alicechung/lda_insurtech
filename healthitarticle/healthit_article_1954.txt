Consumer monitoring device market to reach $47 billion by 2020

June 26, 2014

By Jennifer Bresnick
June 26, 2014 - Wearable health monitoring devices are slated to become much more than a passing fad for the geek contingent according to a new report by MarketsandMarkets, with sensors for patient monitoring, home care, and fitness projected to grow at a CAGR of 5.56% to reach $47.4 billion by 2020.
“Sensing devices are in heavy demand due to their functional capabilities such as processing, data fusion, communication, integration, and control in various applications,” the report says. “The concepts such as home diagnostics and home and remote monitoring are being commercialized with the development in the healthcare equipment such as portability, reduced size, and low-cost architecture facilitating disposable equipment.”
Technologies such as blood glucose sensors, heart rate and blood pressure devices, pedometers, and even posture and motion sensors, are included in the market segment, representing a large portion of the wearable tech that is currently attracting consumer and provider attention across the healthcare spectrum.
